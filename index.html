About Us

The Tang Laboratory at Howard University College of Medicine investigates the molecular mechanisms that govern virus–host interactions in medically important human viruses. Our research integrates molecular virology, cell biology, and metabolic analysis to understand how pathogens reprogram cellular pathways and to identify novel targets for therapeutic intervention. Current work encompasses Zika virus (ZIKV), Dengue virus (DENV), Hepatitis C virus (HCV), and human cytomegalovirus (HCMV).

Recent Highlights

  • Uses of cardiomyocytes and cardiac organoids for studying ZIKV infection and calcium signaling.
  • Comparative metabolic profiling of ZIKV and DENV infection in human cells.
  • HCV host–pathogen studies identifying novel antiviral target pathways.
  • BAC engineering of HCMV for functional and vector-based applications.
